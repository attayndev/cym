Find the Super-Connectors

By Yan · 4 min read

In every industry, every city, every absurdly niche hobby, there are a handful of people who somehow know everyone. Mention any name and they say "oh, we go way back." You can't figure out when they sleep.

Keith Ferrazzi calls them super-connectors in Never Eat Alone, and his observation is one of the most practical in the book: networks aren't evenly distributed. Most people maintain a few dozen active relationships. Super-connectors maintain thousands — which means one genuine relationship with a super-connector puts you two handshakes from more people than a hundred random coffee meetings.

Where they cluster

Certain jobs mint super-connectors, because the work itself is meeting people:

  • Recruiters — professionally obligated to know everyone, in every function, at every level.
  • Journalists and podcasters — their job is literally collecting interesting people.
  • Restaurateurs and bartenders — the original social network, with better lighting.
  • Fundraisers, event organizers, community managers — connection is the job description.
  • That one friend. You know exactly who I mean. Every friend group ships with one.

How to befriend a switchboard (without being a parasite)

Here's where people get it wrong. Super-connectors have world-class radar for being used — everyone wants something from them, always. So the play is the opposite of extraction:

  • Be useful to them first. Feed a journalist a story. Send a recruiter a candidate. Show up at the restaurant on the slow night, and bring people.
  • Become a good node yourself. Connectors route opportunities toward people who pass them along. Be the wire that conducts, not the socket that only receives.
  • Never burn the referral. When a connector opens a door, walk through it impeccably. Their name is on you now, and they never forget how you handled it.

The long game

The endgame isn't collecting super-connectors like Pokémon. It's slowly becoming one — the person who says "you two should talk" a few times a month. Do that for a few years and one day someone maps their network, finds you in the middle of it, and wonders when you sleep.
