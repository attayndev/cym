The problem

Three weeks later, they're just another name in your phone.

You meet someone. Good conversation. You say you'll follow up. Then life happens.

By the time you remember, you've lost where you met, what you talked about, who introduced you, and what you said you'd send. That's how good relationships go cold — not from a falling-out, just from drift.

How it works

Capture the context.
Catch the moment.

One simple loop: save who they are while you still remember, then get a nudge when there's a natural reason to reach out.

Capture the contact

Ten seconds after you meet: where, who introduced you, what you discussed, what you promised. From your phone book, a QR card, or by hand.

Keep the story

Your contacts app remembers names. Call Your Mom remembers the story — the notes, promises, and details that make a follow-up feel personal months later.

Get the nudge

Birthdays, job changes, promises coming due, or simply too much time passing — a few a day, at the right moment. Never a guilt list.

Follow up like a human

One tap to call, text, email, or WhatsApp — with a drafted note that sounds like you and the context already worked in. No more "just checking in."

The nudge engine

"It's been a year. His birthday is Thursday."

  • Learns the rhythm of each relationship — and notices when it drifts.
  • Watches for occasions worth showing up for: birthdays, new roles, promises coming due.
  • Email sync reads only headers — who and when, never contents — so "last talked" is always true.
