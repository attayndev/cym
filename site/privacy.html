Privacy Policy

Effective July 4, 2026 · Call Your Mom is operated by Attayn Group LLC, doing business as Call Your Mom ("we", "us").

The short version: your relationships are yours. We show no ads, we never sell your data, we read email metadata only (never contents), and everything we store exists to remind you to call people you love. Delete it any time.

What we collect

  • Account details. Your email address, and your name if you sign in with Apple or Google or add it yourself.
  • Relationship data you create. The people you add, the context you write about them (where you met, what you discussed, commitments), interaction history, birthdays, and how often you want to stay in touch. This is the heart of the app, and it is private to your account.
  • Device contacts — only if you turn on contacts sync. Names and contact details from your device address book are imported into your account, and contacts you create in the app can be written back to your device. We never touch your address book without you starting a sync.
  • Email metadata — only if you connect an email account. When you connect Gmail, we read message headers only: who a message was from and to, and when it was sent. We request a Google permission that cannot read message bodies or subject lines even if we wanted to. This is used for exactly one thing: keeping "last contact" accurate so we don't nag you about people you already talked to.
  • Notifications and language. A push token if you enable reminders, and your language preference so notifications arrive in it.

What we do with it

  • Run the product: sync your data across your devices, compute which relationships are cooling, and surface the right moment to reach out.
  • Draft messages when you ask. When you tap for a draft, the relevant context (names, notes, the occasion) is sent to our AI provider to generate it. Drafts are suggestions for you — we never send messages on your behalf.
  • Send the notifications you turned on: birthdays, commitments coming due.

What we will never do

  • No advertising, on any tier — the free tier is not paid for with your attention or your data.
  • No selling or renting your information, ever.
  • No reading your email contents. Headers only, as above.
  • No third-party advertising trackers in the app or on this site.

Google user data

Call Your Mom's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google email metadata is used only to provide the "last contact" feature you see in the app, is never used for advertising, and is never sold. You can disconnect Gmail at any time in Settings, which deletes our stored credentials.

Who processes data for us

We use a small set of service providers to run Call Your Mom, each bound to use data only to provide their service to us: Supabase (database hosting), Cloudflare (website hosting), Expo (push notification delivery), Anthropic (message draft generation), Google (sign-in, and email sync if you connect it), and Apple (sign-in).

Your controls

  • Export everything from Settings → Your data, any time, in JSON.
  • Delete your local data from Settings → Reset.
  • Delete your account and all server-side data in the app (Settings → Your data → Delete account) — deletion is immediate. You can also email support@getcym.app; we complete emailed requests within 30 days.
  • Disconnect email sync from Settings at any time.

Security & retention

Data moves over TLS and is stored with per-user access controls; email-sync credentials are held server-side and are never on your device. We keep your data for as long as you keep your account. When you delete your account, it goes with it.

Children

Call Your Mom is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their data.

Changes & contact

If this policy changes in a way that matters, we'll say so in the app before it takes effect. Questions: support@getcym.app.
